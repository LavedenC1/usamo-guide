## Overview

Polynomials appear often at AIME level. Learn how to relate roots and
coefficients and how to build polynomials with given roots.

## Key Ideas

- A polynomial $P(x)$ can be written as $c_nx^n+c_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+c_1x^1+c_0x^0$.
- A polynomial $P(x)$ can be written as $C\cdot (x-r_1)(x-r_2)\cdots(x-r_n)$.
- Symmetric expressions can often be rewritten in terms of a symmetric basis.

## Core Skills

### Key Terms (Introduction!)

For a polynomial $P$ depending on variables $x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n$, we usually write the polynomial as $P(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$.

- The *degree* of a polynomial $P(x)$ is the highest exponent of a polynomial.
- A *root* $r$ of a polynomial $P(x)$ satisfies $P(r) = 0$.

### Factor Theorem

For a polynomial $P(x)$ with root $r$, then we say $P(r) = 0$. In particular, there exists a polynomial $Q(x)$ such that $P(x) = (x-r)\cdot Q(x)$.

### Two ways to interpret a polynomial

**Roots.** For a polynomial $P(x)$ with roots $r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_n$, we can rewrite 

$$
P(x) = C\cdot (x-r_1)(x-r_2)\cdots (x-r_n)
$$

for a constant $C$.

**Coefficients.** For a polynomial $P(x)$, there exists constants $c_0, c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_n$ such that 

$$
P(x) = c_n\cdot x^n+c_{n-1}\cdot x^{n-1}+\cdots+c_1\cdot x^1 + c_0\cdot x^0.
$$

### Symmetric Sums

**Definition.** (Symmetric Expression) An expression $S(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$ is *symmetric* if for any permutation $\sigma = (\sigma_1, \sigma_2,\ldots, \sigma_n)$ of $(x_1,x_2, \ldots, x_n)$ satisfies

$$
S(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n) = S(\sigma_1, \sigma_2,\ldots, \sigma_n).
$$

In general, any symmetric expression can be written in terms of a symmetric basis. The symmetric basis, in particular, is the expressions that actually occur directly due to Vieta's Theorem. The basis, in particular, is

$$
\begin{aligned}
s_1 &= r_1 + r_2 + r_3 + r_4 + \cdots + r_n \\
s_2 &= r_1r_2 + r_1r_3 + r_1r_4 + r_2r_3 + r_2r_4 + r_3r_4 + \cdots \\
s_3 &= r_1r_2r_3 + r_1r_2r_4 + r_1r_3r_4 + r_2r_3r_4 + \cdots \\
&\vdots \\
s_n &= r_1r_2r_3 \cdots r_n
\end{aligned}
$$

For instance, $a^2+b^2+c^2=(a+b+c)^2 - 2(ab+bc+ca)$ and $\dfrac{1}{a}+\dfrac{1}{b}+\dfrac{1}{c} = \dfrac{ab+bc+ca}{abc}$ are both symmetric expressions.


## Worked Example

If $r$ and $s$ are roots of $x^2-5x+6=0$, compute $r^2+s^2$.

We have $r+s=5$ and $rs=6$. Then $r^2+s^2=(r+s)^2-2rs=25-12=13$.

## More Examples

### Example 1: Polynomial from Roots

Find the monic polynomial with roots $2$ and $-3$.

$(x-2)(x+3)=x^2+x-6$.

### Example 2: Reciprocal Sum

If $r+s=4$ and $rs=3$, find $\frac{1}{r}+\frac{1}{s}$.

$\frac{r+s}{rs}=4/3$.

### Example 3: Factor Theorem

If $p(x)=x^3-4x^2-x+4$, show that $x=1$ is a root and factor $p(x)$.

$p(1)=0$, so $(x-1)$ is a factor. Then $p(x)=(x-1)(x^2-3x-4)$.

## Strategy Checklist

- Test small integer roots first.
- Use Vieta for symmetric expressions.
- Keep polynomials factored when possible.
